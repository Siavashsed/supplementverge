## QUORA
**Answer this question:** How important is the post-workout anabolic window for muscle growth?

The anabolic window exists, but it's far less critical than supplement marketing suggests.

Let me clarify what the research actually shows: Your muscles do experience increased protein synthesis for several hours post-workout-this part is true. However, this window extends far longer than the often-cited 30 minutes. We're talking 24-48 hours in many cases, depending on training intensity and your training status.

What matters overwhelmingly more is your total daily protein intake. If you're consuming adequate protein distributed throughout the day-roughly 0.7-1g per pound of bodyweight-your timing becomes almost irrelevant for most people. Your body doesn't suddenly become unable to use protein if you wait 90 minutes instead of 30 minutes.

Where timing *does* matter: If you train fasted, consuming protein post-workout becomes more beneficial. If you're doing multiple training sessions daily, spacing protein between sessions makes sense. If you're an elite athlete optimizing every marginal gain, then yes, timing optimization is worthwhile.

For the average person in the gym 4-5 times weekly? The priority should be hitting your daily protein target, training hard, and sleeping well. The anabolic window panic is largely a product of supplement industry marketing.
