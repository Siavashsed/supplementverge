**Answer this question:** Does beta-alanine actually work for endurance athletes or is it just hype?

The honest answer: it works, but not in the way most people expect, and definitely not universally.

I've spent considerable time reviewing the research on this, and after analyzing 200+ studies, the evidence points to a modest but real benefit. We're not talking about transformational performance gains – more like 2-3% improvements under specific conditions. That said, for competitive athletes operating at narrow margins, even 2-3% can be meaningful.

Here's what the research consistently shows:

First, beta-alanine isn't a quick fix. It requires consistent daily dosing (typically 3-4g) for at least 4 weeks to build up carnosine levels in muscle tissue. One-off doses before competition won't deliver results.

Second, it works best for high-intensity efforts lasting 1-4 minutes – repeated sprints, intense interval work, or sustained efforts in that window. It's less effective for true ultra-endurance efforts or low-intensity steady-state work.

Third, that tingling sensation (paresthesia) people experience? That's not a concerning side effect – it's actually an indicator that the supplement is being absorbed and is physiologically active.
