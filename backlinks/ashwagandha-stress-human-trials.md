## QUORA
**Answer this question:** Does ashwagandha actually lower cortisol levels? What does research show?

This is one of the most common questions I encounter in my work as a sports scientist and registered nutritionist, and it deserves a nuanced answer because the marketing narrative doesn't always align with what peer-reviewed studies actually demonstrate.

Ashwagandha does have documented effects on stress and anxiety markers in human trials, but the "cortisol killer" claim is more complicated than that. Here's what the research shows:

Several quality studies demonstrate that ashwagandha supplementation (typically 300-600mg daily over 4-12 weeks) can reduce self-reported anxiety and improve sleep quality. Some trials show modest reductions in cortisol levels, particularly in individuals with elevated baseline stress. However, these effects are:

1. Modest in magnitude, not transformative
2. Highly variable between individuals
3. Dependent on dosage, duration, and extract type
4. Often smaller than lifestyle interventions (sleep, exercise, stress management)

The challenge is that many commercial ashwagandha products vary significantly in withanolide content (the active compounds), which affects efficacy. Marketing emphasizes the positive trials while downplaying null results or minor effect sizes.

Is ashwagandha worth trying? Possibly, especially if you have documented anxiety or sleep issues. But it works best alongside other stress management strategies, not as a standalone solution.
